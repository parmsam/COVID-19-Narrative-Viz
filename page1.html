COVID-19 Cases, Deaths, and State Response in Indiana Counties for 2020 (as of June 24, 2020)

      Indiana has a population of 6.73 millions people. Marion County holds the city of Indianapolis (capitol of Indiana) and represents the most populated county in Indiana, with a population approaching one million (964,582 as of 2019).

The first COVID-19 case in Indiana was detected on March 6, 2020 (in Marion County), leading Indiana's Governor to declare a public health emergency. The first COVID-19 death occuring on March 16th. COVID-19 case diagnoses peaked in Marion County on May 5th at 305 cases with deaths peaking most recently on May 20th at 16 deaths.

Below one can see the COVID-19 case trend for Marion County with a black line indicating the rolling 7 day average. One can use dropdown menus to examine the death counts as well as select other Indiana counties. The rolling 7 day average curve indicates a rise is occuring after initiation of stage 4.5 in Marion County. Included are labels with major response events and Indiana Back on Track stages for reopening.

  • A: On March 16, Indiana's governor ordered all bars, restaurants, and nightclubs to close to in-house patrons. On March 19, State of Emergency was to be extended for another 30 days, all K-12 schools (both public and private) were to remain closed, order prohibiting evictions and foreclosures, and utilities prohibited from disconnecting services. Extensions for income taxes and property taxes.

  • B: On March 23, issues stay at home order effective March 25 to April 7. Non-essential businesses ordered to close or allow employees to work from home. Public gatherings limited to 10 people.

  • C: On April 2/3, education superintendent announced that all K-12 schools would provide remote instruction for remainder of 2019-2020 school year and governor extended stay at home order two weeks.

  • D: On May 1, governor announced five-stage Back on Track Plan.
